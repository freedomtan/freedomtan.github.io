        At Tamsui the British Consul and his staff observed the beginning of 
the terror in that seaside town.  Several men were executed near the 
Consulate garden.  A father reported that his son - a middle school boy - had 
been killed, and two of his companions badly wounded by a roving patrol.  
When the father sent an older son to recover the body, that son was seized, 
and neither he nor his brother's body were released to the father until he 
had paid over TY 3000 to the [Chinese] Nationalist soldiers who now 
controlled the town.
        Doctors and nurses working at hospitals and emergency stations heard 
countless stories, and had bloody evidence of their truth lying before 
them....
